 

Research site purpose: 

To be included as a research site within the ucalgary.ca ecosystem, a site must: 

Exist to promote awareness of current research activity to one or more of the following: 

Other researchers (not currently part of the research team) 

Funders and donors 

Industry and community partners 

Study participants 

Media 

Have active support of a current UCalgary faculty member 

Involve more than one person in conducting research (i.e. includes additional people than an individual faculty member) 

“Current research activity” means that new activity has been conducted on the topic within the past calendar year. This activity is to be confirmed on an annual basis. 

Additional people may include principal investigators, post-docs, and graduate students. 

“Other researchers” may include Calgary graduate students, other UCalgary faculty, faculty outside of UCalgary, or researcher within industry. 

 

RE: Internally-focused content 

Even if focused on research, if content is primarily intended for research group members themselves, a research site will not be created. Instead, internal tools for sharing content should be used (i.e. Teams, Sharepoint). 

 

Research sites that center on the following will not be created: 

Individuals 

Clubs, interest groups 

Governance and policy boards/networks 

Facilities and services 

Events and conferences 

Documents and research outputs (theses, presentations) 

 

One rule of thumb: if something requires the words to be capitalized (i.e. it’s a proper name) to represent the focus of a site, it’s likely not going to be a valid research site within the ucalgary.ca ecosystem. 

 

 

Content that can be included in ucalgary.ca research sites: 

Explaining the research: 

Introduction to the overall scope of research 

Summaries areas of focus within the overall scope 

Research context and capabilities: 

Research lab facilities and equipment 

Links to related research 

Research activity and output, such as: 

Publications (often as links to publications) 

News and updates on research studies and activity 

Presentations and concepts 

Resources for public use (worksheets, guidelines, etc) 

Who else is (or can be) involved: 

Funders and donors 

Industry and community partners 

Calls for participants in research 

Calls for new team members and collaborators 

Group member information, including: 

Team member highlights  
(i.e. name, photo, role, links to profiles, brief introductory text) 

Team activity highlights  
(i.e. group photos, event attendance, presentations)  

Team announcements and milestones 

 

The list above is only a suggested list.  

 

At least some of the items above should be included, but all are not required.  

 

If a research site does not contain any of the items above, or content that does exist is out-of-date, incorrect, or not substantive, a research site may be removed. (If site owners are still available, Digital Content will work with research site owners before this would occur.) 

 

Additional content not listed above may also be included, as long as it does not fall under the types of content outlined in the following section. 

 

 

Content that should not be included in research sites: 

Including information outlined below reduces the effectiveness of communicating ongoing research and can also negatively impact the performance of other institutional content. 

 

If found within research sites, Digital Content will ask site owners to adjust their site content. If no response or action occurs within a specified timeframe, Digital Content will make changes to a site to remove the items.

Source of truth of research output 

Primary sources of data 

Research exhibitions,  presentations, and archives 

Products, tools, and applications  

Ucalgary.ca content is not an archive and cannot be relied on as the primary source of truth for research output. 

Research material can (and should) be summarized and recontextualized for research site audiences, but the site cannot serve as the “forever home” of the research output. 

Copies of papers can be included, but links to their published location should also be included. 

If retention is needed over the long-term (after research is completed), exhibitions and products likely need their own external website. 
 

 

Student output 

Student portfolios 

Student showcases 

Avoid within research sites. 

This content can easily become outdated and unmanageable. Student output should be created in external websites managed by students, not UCalgary faculty or staff.
